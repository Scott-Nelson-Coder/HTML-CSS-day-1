Top Three Dogs for families

With many great dog breeds in the world its hard to narrow down what we would call the best dog breeds for families. After doing a lot of research
It has shown with out a doupt there are three that stands above the rest. In this article we will be taking a deap dive into thos breeds and showing
why they have been a favorite for years.

Number 1: Labrador Retriever

Why the labredor Retriever? Well lets dive in! The labrador Retriever is the most popular dog in the United States for a reason. The Breed is very
trainable with a friendly personality, they will work well with their humans and other animal alike. The breed is also very versatile, they can nap
and play with the best of them but are known to be a hunting breed as well. Give them the right training and you could have a hard working breed that
can hunt, track, be for show and many great things.

  • Personality: Friendly and outgoing, Labs play well with others
  • Good with Children: Yes
  • Life Expectancy: 10-12 years
  • Barking Level: Medium

Number 2: Golden Retriever

Golden Retrievers originally came into prominence because of the desire for a medium-sized dog that would do well in wild-fowling, both upland game
and waterfowl. Today, the breed's versatility, intelligence, and agreeable personality suit it for many purposes, and it has become one of the successful,
recognizable, and popular breeds in all areas of competition.

  • Personality: Intelligent, friendly, and devoted.
  • Good with Children: Yes
  • Life Expectancy: 10-12 years
  • Barking Level: Barks When Necessary

Number 3: Beagle

Bred to hunt alongside other dogs, the Beagle is amiable and seldom squabbles with other dogs or pets. It makes both canine and human friends easily. The
breed is outgoing, playful, cheerful, adventurous, and gentle, an entertaining companion for people of any age. Because of its independence and
distractibility, it can be a difficult dog to train. Despite being fairly active outside, Beagles can be calm house dogs. However, the breed has a
tendency to become destructive if left alone too long. Beagles are known to bark, especially when untrained or lonely. They are fairly good watchdogs
but poor protection dogs.

  • Personality: Merry, friendly, and curious
  • Good with Children: Yes
  • Life Expectancy: 10-15 years
  • Barking Level: Likes To Be Vocal
